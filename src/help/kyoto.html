Kyoto, the ancient capital of Japan, shows traditional Japanese time like an Edo period clock (和時計 wadokei).

There are two basic ways to make such a clock: with a fixed dial and a hand that moves at different rates for day and night, or with a variable dial and a constant rate hand. Kyoto allows you to toggle between these two methods using the "Constant hand rate" toggle.

The face here has a gray 24-hour dial so that modern viewers can understand the face better (in particular, how in "variable hand rate" mode the modern hours are closer together on the face). A real Wadokei clock would of course have no such dial.

(Some old Japanese clocks used a moving dial and a fixed pointer; Kyoto does not attempt to model that approach.)
